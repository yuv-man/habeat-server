# MOOD × SPECIFIC FOODS

## gut_brain_axis
serotonin_production: `90–95%` produced in gut (enterochromaffin cells), not brain
  → gut_microbiome health directly influences mood, anxiety, impulse_control
  → disrupted_microbiome → ↑ emotional_reactivity, ↓ stress_resilience
vagus_nerve: primary communication_pathway gut→brain (bidirectional)
  → gut_inflammation signals → ↑ amygdala_sensitivity → ↑ emotional_eating_trigger_sensitivity
key_insight: improving_gut_health → improves emotional_eating_resilience (not just digestion)

## high_impact_mood_foods
fermented_foods:
  examples: [kefir, yogurt_with_live_cultures, kimchi, sauerkraut, miso, tempeh, kombucha]
  mechanism: Lactobacillus + Bifidobacterium → ↑ GABA production → ↓ anxiety
  evidence: 4-week fermented food intervention → 23% ↓ anxiety scores
  dose: `1–2 servings/day` meaningful effect

fatty_fish:
  examples: [salmon, mackerel, sardines, herring, anchovies]
  active: EPA + DHA `1.5–3g/day`
  mechanism: ↓ neuroinflammation, ↑ cell_membrane_fluidity → ↑ receptor_sensitivity
  mood_impact: 26% ↓ depression_symptoms (meta-analysis, n=26 trials)
  practical: `2–3 servings/week` = clinical threshold

dark_chocolate (`70%+ cacao`):
  active: flavonoids, theobromine, phenylethylamine, magnesium
  mechanism: ↑ endorphins, ↑ serotonin, ↑ cerebral_blood_flow
  dose: `20–40g/day` → mood benefit without caloric excess
  ee_note: craving dark_chocolate often = magnesium_deficiency signal, not emotional_eating

leafy_greens:
  examples: [spinach, kale, Swiss_chard, arugula, broccoli]
  active: folate, magnesium, vitamin_K, lutein
  mechanism: folate → SAMe → serotonin + dopamine methylation
  deficiency_link: low_folate → depression_risk ↑ 2×, ↑ rumination

berries:
  examples: [blueberries, strawberries, blackberries, raspberries]
  active: anthocyanins, quercetin, vitamin_C
  mechanism: antioxidant → ↓ oxidative_stress → ↓ cortisol_impact on brain
  blood_glucose: low_GI → slow_glucose_release → stable_mood

## stress-specific food protocols
acute_stress:
  immediate: magnesium_rich (dark_chocolate, nuts) + slow_carb (oats, sweet_potato)
  avoid: caffeine (amplifies cortisol), alcohol (false_calm → rebound_anxiety), refined_sugar (spike→crash)
  rationale: cortisol depletes magnesium → magnesium replenishment ↓ cortisol response

chronic_stress:
  priority: [omega3_fish, adaptogenic_foods, B_vitamin_rich]
  adaptogenic: ashwagandha_extract, turmeric+piperine, green_tea (L-theanine)
  L-theanine: `100–200mg` → calm_alertness without sedation, ↑ alpha_brain_waves
  practical: matcha_tea = caffeine + L-theanine (balanced_stimulation vs coffee_alone)

sadness_low_mood:
  priority: [tryptophan_sources + carb_co-ingestion, vitamin_D (sun/food), omega3]
  tryptophan_sources: [turkey, eggs, salmon, dairy, tofu, pumpkin_seeds, bananas]
  co-ingestion_rule: tryptophan + complex_carb in same meal → 3× better serotonin_conversion
  vitamin_D_foods: [fatty_fish, egg_yolks, fortified_dairy, mushrooms_UV_exposed]

anxiety:
  priority: [magnesium, GABA_supporting_foods, fermented_foods, chamomile]
  GABA_support: fermented_foods, whole_grains, green_tea
  avoid: caffeine (>200mg/day ↑ anxiety), alcohol, high_sodium (↑ blood_pressure → ↑ anxiety_sensation)

tiredness_fatigue:
  iron_sources (if deficient): [lean_red_meat, lentils, spinach + vitamin_C, fortified_cereals]
  B12: [meat, fish, dairy, eggs, fortified_plant_milks]
  complex_carbs: sustained_energy vs simple_sugar_crash
  hydration: `1–2%` dehydration → measurable ↓ cognitive_performance, ↑ fatigue_perception

## foods_that_amplify_emotional_eating_risk
refined_sugar: dopamine_spike → tolerance → craving_cycle (similar mechanism to addictive_substances)
ultra_processed_foods: engineered for hyperpalatability → override satiety_signals
alcohol: ↓ prefrontal_cortex → ↓ inhibitory_control → ↑ impulsive_eating
artificial_sweeteners: confuse gut_microbiome signals, may ↑ sweet_cravings long-term
caffeine_excess (>400mg/day): ↑ cortisol, ↑ anxiety → ↑ ee_trigger_sensitivity
