# CBT INTERVENTIONS FOR EMOTIONAL EATING

## urge_surfing
origin: Marlatt 1994 (addiction), adapted for EE by Linehan (DBT)
mechanism: cravings are waves — peak at `10–20min` then subside without action
  observation_without_action → weakens_neural_pathway over time (extinction_learning)
protocol:
  1. notice_urge → name_it ("I notice a craving for X")
  2. locate_in_body (where do you feel it physically?)
  3. observe_without_acting (breathe, watch it change)
  4. ride_peak (`10–15min`) without eating
  5. notice_natural_subsidence
efficacy: 6-week practice → 40% ↓ EE episodes (Telch 2001)
dose: daily practice when craving present | not emergency technique alone
contraindication: not_suitable during acute_restrictive episodes (need food first)

## hunger_check (pre-eating pause)
protocol: before eating, rate hunger 1–10 (see emotional-eating.md hunger_scale)
  if < 3: hunger_not_physical → explore emotion before eating
  if 3–7: proceed, eat mindfully
  if > 7: already_overfull → examine what overrode satiety signal
decision_pause: `3 deep breaths` before any unplanned eating → activates prefrontal_cortex
  mechanism: breaks_automatic_response, allows conscious_choice
efficacy: 10sec pause before eating → ↓ consumption by 20% in triggered_states

## mindful_eating (Kristeller MEAL protocol)
not: eating_slowly or food_rules
is: full_present_moment_awareness during eating
protocol:
  1. pre-meal: rate hunger, name current_emotion
  2. observe food (5sec): colors, textures, smells
  3. first bite: full_attention, no_distraction
  4. mid-meal check-in: hunger rating, still enjoying?
  5. stop_at_6: satisfaction, not fullness
  6. post-meal: rate emotion (better/same/worse vs pre-meal)
efficacy: 6-week MEAL program → 58% ↓ binge_eating, 40% ↓ emotional_eating
app_integration: mood_before + mood_after logging = data-driven mindful_eating

## chain_analysis (DBT)
purpose: map full sequence from trigger to EE_episode to identify intervention_points
steps:
  A: vulnerability_factor (tired? hungry? stressed? isolated?)
  B: triggering_event (specific situation or emotion)
  C: links (thoughts, feelings, body_sensations leading to eating)
  D: behavior (what/how much eaten)
  E: consequences (short_term + long_term)
insight: most_effective_intervention is earliest_in_chain (vulnerability or trigger stage)
  → profile agent: identify vulnerability patterns (time, day, emotional state)

## opposite_action (DBT)
mechanism: doing opposite of emotion-driven urge → breaks reinforcement_loop
EE application:
  urge: eat (comfort) → opposite: movement, social_connection, self-care
  urge: isolate with food → opposite: reach_out to one_person
  urge: binge → opposite: structured_small_meal + presence
criteria: use when emotion doesn't fit facts OR when acting on emotion makes things worse
efficacy: opposite_action practiced 3×/week → 33% ↓ EE at 12_weeks

## stimulus_control
premise: remove environmental_triggers = remove decision_requirement
strategies:
  out_of_sight_foods: ↓ consumption by 54% (Wansink 2006)
  kitchen_closed_time: set ritual end_time → removes decision in vulnerable window
  single_eating_location: eating only at table → breaks location-based conditioned_cues
  no_phone_while_eating: ↓ meal_awareness → ↑ overconsumption by 25%
  grocery_list + full_stomach shopping: ↓ impulse_purchase of trigger_foods

## behavioral_activation_for_EE
theory: EE often fills function that activity/connection/achievement should fill
assessment: what does eating provide? (comfort, stimulation, reward, distraction, connection)
replace_with_functionally_equivalent:
  comfort → warm_bath, soft_blanket, favourite_music, self-compassion_practice
  stimulation → novel_activity, phone_call, short_walk, creative_task
  reward → non-food_reward list (user_specific)
  distraction → physical_activity (5–10min walk → cortisol ↓ 15%)
  connection → text/call someone instead of "eating together alone"

## self-compassion (Neff 2003, Gilbert 2010)
critical_for_EE: shame_and_self_criticism perpetuate the cycle (see emotional-eating.md EE_cycle)
three_components:
  self_kindness: treat_self as you would a good_friend
  common_humanity: "I'm not uniquely broken — many people struggle with this"
  mindfulness: acknowledge_difficulty without over-identification
protocol (self-compassion break):
  "This is a moment of suffering." (acknowledge)
  "Suffering is part of being human." (common_humanity)
  Hand on heart → "May I be kind to myself." (self_kindness)
  `3min` practice → interrupts shame_spiral
efficacy: 8-week self-compassion training → 35% ↓ EE + 28% ↓ body_shame

## 5-4-3-2-1 grounding (anxiety/acute_stress EE)
use_when: acute_anxiety → food_urge
protocol: name 5_things_you_see, 4_you_can_touch, 3_you_hear, 2_you_smell, 1_you_taste
mechanism: sensory_engagement → prefrontal_cortex reactivation, amygdala_calming
duration: `60–90sec` → measurable ↓ subjective_anxiety
advantage: discrete, portable, invisible, zero_cost
