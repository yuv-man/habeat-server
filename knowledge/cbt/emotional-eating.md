# EMOTIONAL EATING — MECHANISMS & TRIGGERS

## definition
emotional_eating (EE): consumption of food in response to emotional states rather than physiological hunger
  prevalence: 30–49% of adults report regular EE
  gender: more_prevalent in women (60% vs 35% in men) but equally_harmful in both
  not_disorder_alone: exists on spectrum → clinical_BED at severe end

## the_EE_cycle
trigger (emotion/situation)
→ urge_to_eat (automatic, pre-conscious)
→ eating_episode (often fast, large_quantity, high_palatability foods)
→ temporary_relief (dopamine, serotonin, comfort)
→ secondary_emotions (guilt, shame, disgust, self-criticism)
→ negative_affect ↑ (worse than pre-eating)
→ next_trigger_sensitized (cycle_perpetuates)
key_insight: food is effective short-term emotion_regulator → that's why cycle persists, not weakness

## trigger_taxonomy
### negative_affect_triggers (most common, ~68% of EE episodes)
stress: cortisol↑ → cravings for high_fat, high_sugar | peak window: 2–4h post_stressor
  physiological: HPA_axis activation → ghrelin↑, NPY↑ → appetite_for_comfort_foods
anxiety: hypervigilance → food as distraction, control, or self-soothing
sadness / low_mood: ↓ dopamine → food as dopamine_substitute | ↑ preference for sweet_foods
loneliness: social_hunger → food as comfort proxy | ↑ in isolated living situations
boredom: low_arousal → food as stimulation | often_automatic (not craving-based)
anger / frustration: oral_aggression component, fast_eating, often_followed by guilt_spiral

### external_triggers (~22% of EE episodes)
food_cues: visual/smell exposure → cue-induced_craving independent of hunger_state
social_situations: group_eating → ↑ consumption 30–48% vs eating_alone
specific_locations: couch, car, desk → conditioned_cue → automatic_eating
time_of_day: fixed_time triggers (e.g. 10pm) → habitual_eating without hunger
advertisements / social_media_food_content: 20min exposure → ↑ calorie_intake 25%

### cognitive_triggers (~10% of EE episodes)
"diet starts Monday" → permission_granting → pre-emptive_overeating
all-or-nothing slip: one_deviation → "ruined it" → abandonment_eating
body_negative_thought → shame → comfort_eating
comparison to others → inadequacy → soothing_eating

## physiological_vs_emotional_hunger_distinction
| dimension | physical_hunger | emotional_hunger |
|---|---|---|
| onset | gradual (`30–60min`) | sudden |
| location | stomach, physical_sensation | mouth, mind, "craving" |
| food_specificity | open to variety | specific (usually high_palatability) |
| satiety | stops when full | continues despite fullness |
| time_since_last_meal | `3h+` | irrelevant |
| emotional_context | neutral | preceded by emotional_event |
| post-eating | satisfied | guilt, shame, emptiness |

## hunger_scale (clinical tool)
1–2: physical_discomfort, light_headed (too_hungry → high_ee_risk)
3–4: genuinely_hungry (optimal eating window)
5–6: neutral / satisfied (stop here)
7–8: full (discomfort beginning)
9–10: overfull, physical_pain
ee_pattern: eating below 3 or above 6 habitually → strong_ee_indicator

## neurological_underpinning
reward_system: highly_palatable_foods → dopamine_release in nucleus_accumbens (same pathway as drugs)
stress_response: amygdala_activation → suppresses prefrontal_cortex → ↓ rational decision_making
habit_loop: trigger → routine (eating) → reward → neural_pathway_strengthened over_time
  implication: EE is partly_habit, partly_emotion_regulation → both need addressing

## EE_severity_markers (for profile classification)
low: occasional (<1×/week), specific triggers, recognizes during episode, mild_guilt
medium: 2–4×/week, multiple triggers, recognition after episode, moderate_impact on diet_goals
high: daily+, broad triggers, ↓ recognition during episode, significant_impact, possible_NES
